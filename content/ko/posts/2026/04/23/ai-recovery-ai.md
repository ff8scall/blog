## 핵심 요약
- 애플이 팀 쿡의 후계자로 존 터너스를 낙점, AI 분야의 지체 현상을 타개하려 함.
- 시리(Siri) 개편이 수차례 지연되는 등 애플의 AI 모델 성능은 경쟁사보다 최소 1년 뒤처진 상태.
- 터너스는 폴더블 아이폰, 스마트 글래스 등 차세대 하드웨어와 AI의 결합이라는 중책을 맡음.
- English Analysis:
- Apple stands at a critical juncture. For the first time in the Post-Jobs era, the company’s reputation for being "fashionably late but superior" is under existential threat. The news of John Ternus taking a central role in CEO transition planning signals a desire to return to a more decisive, product-focused leadership style. Bloomberg frames this as a bet on "Jobs-era decisiveness"—a quality Apple desperately needs as it struggles to catch up in a generative AI race where it currently lacks a frontier-class model.
- The internal state of Apple’s AI efforts is deeply concerning. Reports indicate that Apple’s proprietary LLMs trail year-old versions of GPT-4 and Claude by a significant margin. Even more damaging is the trajectory of Siri; the long-promised overhaul has reportedly been pushed back three times since 2024 due to technical debt and architectural conflicts. While "Apple Intelligence" was announced with much fanfare, it has yet to gain the "cool factor" or utility of ChatGPT, often feeling like a set of disparate features rather than a cohesive AI OS. This delay has allowed rivals like Samsung and Google to claim the "AI Phone" narrative first.
- John Ternus, known for his instrumental work on the transition to Apple Silicon and the redesign of the Mac, inherits a complex and risky product roadmap. His challenge isn't just about silicon and screens; it’s about the soul of the software. To succeed, Ternus will likely need to centralize decision-making, cutting through the layers of corporate bureaucracy that have slowed Apple’s AI response. He must oversee the integration of generative AI into a rumored foldable iPhone and a pair of smart glasses—devices where AI is not just a feature, but the primary interface.
