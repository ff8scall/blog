## Strategic Deep-Dive

## Key Technologies at the Beijing Auto Show

The 2026 Beijing Auto Show cemented its position as a global stage showcasing China's electric vehicle (EV) industry leading worldwide technological standards. Companies like BYD and Xiaomi have moved beyond simple price competitiveness, astonishing the world with second-generation solid-state batteries maximizing energy density and ultra-fast charging systems capable of full charges in just 8 minutes. Xiaomi's new SU9 model, in particular, demonstrated the pinnacle of the "Software-Defined Vehicle (SDV)," where the car becomes a massive smart device.

Its AI assistant, which learns driver habits in real-time and seamlessly integrates with home appliances, has created a gap that traditional combustion engine manufacturers cannot hope to close.

## Evolution of China's EV Export Strategy

To overcome fierce competition in the domestic market, Chinese EV brands are now targeting the global premium market. Vehicles unveiled at this auto show boast high-end interiors and cutting-edge autonomous driving features that rival those of European luxury brands. Chinese companies have completely shed their "low-cost image" and are aiming squarely at the high-end market, which can generate high profits.

To this end, they are moving away from simply exporting finished vehicles and aiming to directly break through trade barriers by establishing local factories and vertically integrating their supply chains.

## Global Trade Barriers and Responses

Despite the urgent situation of rising tariff barriers in the US and Europe, the atmosphere at the Beijing Auto Show was vibrant. Chinese manufacturers demonstrated overwhelming technological prowess, suggesting that political regulations cannot stop the flow of innovation. They are responding to the "Decoupling" of supply chains by securing production bases in Europe and preemptively capturing the Southeast Asian market.

The 2026 Beijing Auto Show became a historic venue proclaiming the complete transfer of hegemony in the global automotive industry from Western engines to Eastern batteries and software.

**KOR_INSIGHT (Translated):**

While China's dominance in EVs is built on the powerful weapon of supply chain vertical integration, this simultaneously becomes the core spark for global trade conflicts. Particularly, as "data security" emerges as a new trade barrier, Western countries' aversion to Chinese software will intensify. Unless Chinese companies secure security reliability and transparency that meets global standards beyond technological superiority, their leap may only be a "half-success." The next five years will be a 'war of trust,' not a technology war.
