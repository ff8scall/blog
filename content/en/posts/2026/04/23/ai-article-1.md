## Strategic Deep-Dive

## Background of the Partnership

NEC, a leading Japanese IT and telecommunications company, has entered into a strategic alliance with Anthropic, a global AI leader, marking one of the most significant turning points in Japan's technology industry in 2026. The core driver behind this collaboration is the surging demand for "Sovereign AI" within Japan. The Japanese government and critical industries such as finance and healthcare are increasingly seeking to leverage world-class generative AI performance while safeguarding data sovereignty.

By integrating Anthropic's Claude models into its secure, closed-source cloud infrastructure, NEC has successfully built a safe enterprise AI environment free from external data leaks.

## Changes in the Enterprise AI Market

The 'Constitutional AI' framework proposed by Anthropic is perfectly synergistic with the prudent and ethical corporate culture of Japan. Unlike existing general-purpose models that have struggled with uncontrollable outputs, NEC's Anthropic-based solutions are designed to strictly adhere to internal corporate regulations and legal guidelines. As of 2026, Japanese companies are demanding sophisticated AI assistants that go beyond simple chatbots, capable of complex legal document analysis, advanced coding support, and perfect understanding of Japanese honorifics and business grammar.

NEC is transforming the market landscape by meeting these high-performance needs through this partnership.

## Future Prospects and Competitive Landscape

Currently, the Japanese AI market is fiercely competitive, with SoftBank's ultra-large model investment and NTT's 'tsuzumi' model vying for dominance. NEC has adopted a pragmatic approach by collaborating with Anthropic, reducing the enormous costs and time associated with developing its own model, while immediately deploying global-level cutting-edge AI technology on-site. This has resulted in a dramatic acceleration of digital transformation (DX) in the public sector.

However, in the long term, the key to winning the survival battle against global Big Tech lies in reducing reliance on overseas technologies, combining them with Japan's unique industrial data, and firmly establishing NEC's own independent application layer.
