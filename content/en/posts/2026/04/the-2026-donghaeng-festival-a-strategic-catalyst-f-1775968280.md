In an era defined by geopolitical volatility and the looming specter of a global economic slowdown, the South Korean Ministry of SMEs and Startups (MSS) has inaugurated a strategic counter-offensive. The '2026 Donghaeng Festival,' launched in Jeonju, is a sophisticated, state-led intervention designed to decentralize consumer activity and provide a vital liquidity injection into regional commerce. It acts as a macroeconomic stabilizer, incentivizing domestic tourism to bridge the gap between metropolitan hubs and regional markets. By leveraging 'experiential consumption,' the initiative encourages small-to-medium enterprises (SMEs) to adopt digital agility while preserving their unique localized value. Ultimately, the festival serves as a bellwether for the survival of small-scale commercial ecosystems, pushing traditional retailers toward a modernized O2O hybrid model in an increasingly high-uncertainty marketplace.
