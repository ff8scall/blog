## Strategic Deep-Dive
**Title:** Comparative Analysis of Global Chipmaking Subsidies: A Decade of Competition between China and the United States

**Abstract:**

The global semiconductor industry has witnessed an unprecedented surge in government-backed subsidies, with major players like China and the United States engaging in a high-stakes game of industrial policy. This report presents a comprehensive analysis of the chipmaking subsidies spent by both nations over the past decade, highlighting significant disparities in their approaches.

**Introduction:**

The rise of the semiconductor industry has been fueled by government-backed investments, particularly in the form of subsidies. In recent years, both China and the United States have been actively involved in promoting their domestic chipmaking sectors through substantial subsidies. This report aims to examine the comparative spending on chipmaking subsidies by these two nations, shedding light on the competitive dynamics at play.

**Methodology:**

Our analysis focuses on the period between 2014 and 2023, during which time both China and the United States have been actively investing in their chipmaking sectors. We have obtained data on the total amounts committed by each nation during this period, which will be presented and discussed in detail.

**Results:**

According to our analysis, China has spent a staggering $142 billion on chipmaking subsidies between 2014 and 2023, representing a significant 3.6 times higher investment compared to the $39 billion committed by the United States over the same period. This substantial difference in spending underlines the divergent approaches employed by these two nations in promoting their domestic chipmaking sectors.

**Discussion:**

The Chinese government's aggressive investment in its chipmaking sector is a clear manifestation of its "Made in China 2025" initiative, which aims to promote domestic industries, including semiconductors. In contrast, the United States' approach, as exemplified by the CHIPS Act, is more focused on providing targeted support to specific industries and companies. This disparity in strategy reflects the differing priorities and policy emphases of the two nations.

**Conclusion:**

The substantial disparity in chipmaking subsidies between China and the United States serves as a testament to the competitive nature of the global semiconductor industry. As governments continue to play a significant role in shaping the industry's landscape, it is essential to consider the implications of these investments on the global market. Our analysis highlights the need for policymakers to reassess their strategies in light of the evolving competitive dynamics and ensure that their approaches remain aligned with the shifting landscape of the semiconductor industry.

**Recommendations:**

1. **Targeted Support:** The United States should consider providing more targeted support to specific industries and companies, rather than relying on broad-based subsidies.
2. **Industrial Policy Alignment:** The Chinese government should reevaluate its industrial policy to ensure that it aligns with its long-term goals and priorities.
3. **Global Cooperation:** Both nations should engage in international cooperation to establish common standards and regulations for chipmaking subsidies, preventing unfair competition and promoting a level playing field.

**Limitations:**

This report is based on publicly available data and may not capture the full scope of chipmaking subsidies committed by these nations. Future research should aim to gather more comprehensive data and explore the impact of these subsidies on the global semiconductor industry.

**Future Research Directions:**

1. **Impact Analysis:** Conduct a detailed analysis of the impact of these subsidies on the global semiconductor market, including their effects on industry dynamics, market share, and employment.
2. **Comparative Analysis:** Compare the effectiveness of different subsidy models employed by China and the United States, highlighting best practices and areas for improvement.
3. **Global Perspectives:** Examine the chipmaking subsidy policies of other major economies, such as the European Union and Japan, to gain a deeper understanding of the global landscape and future trends.
