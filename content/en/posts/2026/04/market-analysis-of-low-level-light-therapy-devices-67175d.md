## 💡 2. Technological Deep Dive: Photobiomodulation (LED Therapy)

### 2.1 Mechanism of Action
iRestore devices operate based on the principle of Low-Level Light Therapy (LLLT). This technology involves delivering specific bands of visible light energy (typically in the red and near-infrared spectrum) to the scalp.

*   **Red Spectrum (630–670 nm):** Wavelengths in this range are known to penetrate the dermal layers, stimulating mitochondrial function within the hair follicle cells (dermal papilla). This stimulation increases ATP (adenosine triphosphate) production, which is the primary energy source for cellular growth.
*   **Near-Infrared Spectrum (810–860 nm):** These deeper penetrating wavelengths help improve vascularity, promoting enhanced nutrient and oxygen delivery to the root structures.

### 2.2 Reported Efficacy
The devices are designed to stimulate the hair growth cycle by encouraging the transition of follicles from the resting (telogen) phase back into the active (anagen) phase. While results are highly dependent on user compliance, device quality, and underlying biological factors, the technology is positioned as a clinically supported, non-invasive therapeutic modality.
