## Strategic Deep-Dive
The war in Ukraine is rapidly accelerating the development of autonomous lethal systems, pushing the technology toward a terrifying "inflection point." Engineer Yaroslav Azhnyuk provides a chilling technical vision of this future: swarms of autonomous drones designed to carry and protect other drones, all engaged in a high-speed ballet of interception and attack. These systems are governed by "AI agents" that act under the strategic oversight of an "AI General," removing the need for real-time human commands. This architecture is designed to overcome the latency and jamming vulnerabilities of remote-controlled systems.

Furthermore, Azhnyuk describes flotillas of autonomous submarines capable of carrying hundreds of smaller sub-units, extending the autonomous battlefield to the maritime domain. Technically, this represents a shift toward "swarming intelligence," where decentralized algorithms allow individual units to coordinate complex tactical maneuvers, such as sacrificial decoys or coordinated pincer strikes, at speeds no human operator could manage. The engineering goal has transitioned from building better drones to building smarter swarms that maintain mission integrity even after heavy losses.

This dystopian shift toward fully autonomous combat raises existential questions about the laws of war and the future of human control over lethal force.
