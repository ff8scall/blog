## Executive Summary
["Intel has hired Shawn 'Seung Hoon' Han, a former Samsung Foundry executive with three decades of experience, to lead its Foundry Services division. This strategic move aims to transform Intel's foundry business from a technology-focused entity into a customer-centric organization capable of winning long-term contracts for its 18A and 14A nodes."]

## Strategic Deep-Dive
Intel’s appointment of Shawn Han as Senior Vice President and General Manager of Foundry Services signifies a maturation of the Intel Foundry strategy. Han, who previously oversaw sales at Samsung Foundry and has 30 years of experience in logic process nodes since 1996, is tasked with bridging the gap between Intel's technical roadmap and actual market adoption. Having joined Samsung Foundry from Samsung Semiconductor in 2021, Han brings deep insights into how pure-play foundries manage customer obsession.

While Intel has promoted its 18A and 14A nodes as technologically superior, it faces the challenge of shedding its rigid "IDM culture" in favor of "foundry discipline." Han’s role will involve managing customer relations and sales to secure multi-year deals that require predictable Process Design Kits (PDKs), stable yields, and responsive support. This hire follows the departure of Kevin O’Buckley to Qualcomm and indicates Intel’s focus on gaining the trust of external fabless giants to move beyond "interest" into "booked wafer volume."
