## Strategic Deep-Dive
TSMC’s Q1 2026 financial performance marks a pivotal shift in the semiconductor landscape, with revenue reaching $35.9 billion, a 40.6% increase year-over-year. The High-Performance Computing (HPC) segment now commands 61% of total revenue, illustrating the massive scale of the AI hardware build-out. For the first time, Nvidia has been identified as the foundry's largest customer, accounting for 19% of 2025 revenue, driven by the dominance of Blackwell architecture, while Apple fell to 17%.

In response to "tight" capacity, TSMC is aggressively expanding its N3 (3nm) footprint. Key expansions include a new module in Tainan (1H 2027), the clarification of Fab 21 Phase 2 in Arizona (2H 2027) where a second plot of land has been acquired, and a strategic upgrade of Fab 23 in Japan to 3nm (2028). Technologically, 5nm nodes accounted for 36% of revenue, 3nm for 25%, and 7nm for 7%.

While N2 chips are being mass-produced, revenue is not yet recognized. Management is also converting N5 tools to N3 and maintaining "flexible capacity" across N7, N5, and N3 nodes. Despite a high gross margin of 66.2%, management warned that the Middle East conflict could drive up the cost of essential chemicals and gases, impacting future profitability.

The company has raised its annual revenue growth projection to over 30%, with a CapEx budget now spanning $52 billion to $56 billion.
