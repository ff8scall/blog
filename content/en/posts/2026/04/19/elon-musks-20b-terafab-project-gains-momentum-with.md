## Strategic Deep-Dive
Elon Musk’s Terafab project is moving at "light speed," with staff aggressively soliciting quotes from suppliers like Applied Materials, Tokyo Electron, and Lam Research. In one instance, a company was contacted during a Friday holiday for an estimate due the following Monday, reflecting Musk's relentless pace. Launched in March 2026, Terafab is backed by a $20 billion seed investment, though experts estimate total costs could hit $5 trillion.

The facility's output is earmarked for space-based AI datacenters (80%) and Tesla’s Optimus robots and vehicles (20%). A significant boost came from Intel, which announced it would help refactor fab technology for the project, propelling Intel's market cap to its highest in 25 years. Financial markets reacted positively, with Tokyo Electron jumping 5.3%.

However, industry veterans remain skeptical. Nvidia’s Jensen Huang emphasized that advanced manufacturing is an "artistry" that transcends money, while TSMC CEO C.C. Wei noted that fab construction and ramp-up take at least 3-5 years with "no shortcuts."
