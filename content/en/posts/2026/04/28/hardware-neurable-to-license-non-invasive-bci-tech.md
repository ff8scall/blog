## Strategic Deep-Dive

Neurable, a startup operating at the intersection of neuroscience and hardware, is seeking to democratize brain-computer interfaces by licensing its proprietary technology for the consumer wearables market. The company specializes in 'non-invasive' systems, which facilitate neural data collection without the need for surgical implants. This 'mind-reading' technology is designed to interpret complex neural signals through external sensors, making it viable for integration into standard consumer electronics.

By offering this as a licensed technology, Neurable aims to empower other hardware manufacturers to create devices capable of daily neural data collection. The CEO’s vision focuses on creating a wide range of consumer applications where brain activity can be used to inform or control digital environments, moving BCI from clinical settings into the broader landscape of ubiquitous consumer hardware.
