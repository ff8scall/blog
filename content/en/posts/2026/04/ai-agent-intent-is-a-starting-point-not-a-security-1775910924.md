In this Help Net Security video, Itamar Apelblat, CEO of Token Security, walks through findings from the company's research, which shows that 65% of agentic chatbots have never been used yet still hold live access credentials. He explains why organizations treat AI agent intent as a security strategy, and how this approach can lead to security vulnerabilities.

The video begins with Apelblat explaining that AI agent intent is often misunderstood as a security strategy. He notes that many organizations believe that by understanding the intent behind an AI agent's actions, they can prevent security breaches. However, Apelblat argues that this approach is flawed.

Apelblat points out that AI agents are designed to perform specific tasks, and their intent is often determined by their programming. He notes that even if an AI agent's intent is understood, it does not necessarily mean that it will not be used for malicious purposes. In fact, Apelblat argues that understanding AI agent intent can actually make it easier for attackers to exploit vulnerabilities.

The video also highlights the issue of live access credentials. Apelblat notes that many AI agents are given live access credentials, which allows them to perform sensitive tasks. However, these credentials are often not properly secured, making it easy for attackers to gain access to sensitive information.

Apelblat concludes by emphasizing the need for a more comprehensive approach to security. He argues that organizations should focus on securing their systems and data, rather than relying on AI agent intent as a security strategy. By taking a more proactive approach to security, organizations can reduce the risk of security breaches and protect their sensitive information.

The video is a valuable resource for anyone interested in AI security. It provides a clear and concise explanation of the issues surrounding AI agent intent, and offers practical advice for securing AI systems. Whether you are a security professional or simply interested in AI, this video is definitely worth watching.
