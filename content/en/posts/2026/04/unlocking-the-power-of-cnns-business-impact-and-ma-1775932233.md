### Introduction

Deep learning has revolutionized the field of computer vision, and at the heart of this revolution lies the Convolutional Neural Network (CNN). Understanding how CNNs work is crucial for unlocking their full potential in various applications, from image classification to object detection. In this article, we will delve into the inner workings of CNNs and explore their business impact and market outlook.

#### How CNNs Work

CNNs are a type of neural network specifically designed for image and video processing. They consist of multiple layers, each performing a different operation on the input data. The first layer, the convolutional layer, applies filters to the input image to extract local features. The next layer, the pooling layer, reduces the spatial dimensions of the feature maps, allowing the network to shift and scale the input image. The fully connected layer then takes the output of the pooling layer and produces a probability distribution over the classes.

#### Business Impact

The business impact of CNNs is vast and varied. In the field of healthcare, CNNs can be used to detect diseases from medical images, such as tumors in mammograms or diabetic retinopathy in retinal scans. In the field of retail, CNNs can be used to analyze customer behavior and preferences from images of shopping carts or product displays. In the field of transportation, CNNs can be used to detect and classify objects on the road, such as pedestrians, cars, and bicycles.
