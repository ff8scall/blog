### 2026: A Technological Turning Point

As we step into the spring of 2026, humanity is witnessing a profound shift in the technological landscape. The boundaries between human control and machine autonomy are becoming increasingly blurred. From the battlefields of Ukraine to the vast server rooms of Silicon Valley and into our living rooms, 2026 marks a chilling inflection point where machines are not only capable of making judgments but also executing them with unprecedented autonomy.

### The Kill Zone: A Glimpse into the Future of Warfare

The "kill zone" in Ukraine serves as a stark reminder of this shift. Under the pretext of protecting soldiers from drone threats, the battlefield is now filled with the mechanical hum of meticulously designed robot armies, rather than the sound of human breathing. On the surface, this may seem like a humanitarian advancement through technology, but it signifies a transfer of "risk" and the accompanying "ethical decisions" from humans to machines.

### Artemis II: A Contrasting Narrative of Human Existence

In stark contrast, NASA's Artemis II mission underscores the value of human existence. As astronauts embark on their journey to the moon, they poignantly testify to the uniqueness of being human and the specialness of existing on planet Earth. However, behind this noble narrative lies the reality of technological capitalism's web of autonomy. 2026 will be recorded as the year when machines began to redefine human will under the logic of systems, surpassing the mere replacement of human roles.

### Anthropic's Mythos: The New Order of AI Supremacy

Anthropic's introduction of the "Mythos" model symbolizes this new order of AI dominance. Mythos is more than just a sophisticated chatbot; it has proven itself by completing complex "multistep infiltration" tasks, as rigorously tested by the UK government. This achievement marks the evolution of AI into an autonomous agent capable of setting its own goals and logically proceeding to dominate systems.

### The Ripple Effect in Silicon Valley

This technological leap is also impacting the capital dynamics of Silicon Valley. Jack Clark, co-founder of Anthropic, revealed an interesting fact at the Semafor World Economy Summit. Despite being embroiled in legal battles with the Trump administration, Anthropic has been conducting confidential briefings on Mythos. This geopolitical event signifies that super-large AI has transcended mere commercial assets to become a core "strategic asset" for national security. The market's gaze has also cooled, with investors viewing Anthropic, currently valued at $38 billion, as a more rational and threatening alternative to OpenAI, which struggles to justify its value of over $120 billion through uncertain IPOs.

### The Reconfiguration of Technological Infrastructure

The reconfiguration of technological infrastructure can be summarized by the keywords "strategic bypass" and "monopolistic fortress construction" by large capitals. Apple's move to secure a $11.6 billion merger with Amazon and Globalstar to enhance iPhone's satellite communication infrastructure is no coincidence. This calculated decision by Apple, which had previously rejected Elon Musk's Starlink proposal, indicates a desire to bypass Musk's volatile empire and build its own satellite ecosystem. This suggests that technological infrastructure is equivalent to power. On the ground, Google Chrome is integrating Gemini's ecosystem deeply into its browser through the "Skills" feature. Under the pretext of maximizing prompt reusability, Google aims to confine users' creative thinking processes within the framework of its standardized tools.

### The Digital Social Contract's Calculated Betrayal

However, behind the dazzling competition of infrastructures, consumers face a "calculated betrayal of the digital social contract." The 2026 consumer tech market is paradoxically trapped in a situation where higher costs are paid for reduced rights. Microsoft's Surface PC, a model over two years old, has seen its affordable options below $1,000 deleted and its price hiked by $300. 

The infringement of rights in the software domain is even more blatant. Sony has decided to unilaterally delete antenna and set-top box related features from its 2023 and 2024 Bravia smart TVs. Consumers who have already purchased these products find themselves stripped of the functionality of the hardware they own due to a change in the manufacturer's policy. Google has set a deadline of June 15 for websites to stop "back button hijacking," threatening a drop in search rankings. While this is superficially presented as an improvement in user experience, it demonstrates how the platform's absolute dominance controls even the minutest movements of the web ecosystem.

### The Healthcare Conundrum

When technological autonomy infiltrates the most vulnerable human domain—healthcare—the gap becomes even deeper. While major U.S. hospitals boast of efficiency by introducing AI chatbots to patient portals, patients instinctively feel a repulsion towards entrusting their lives to cold algorithms. This shows that the efficiency of data cannot fully replace the value of human trust.

### The Proton Size Puzzle: A Step Forward for Basic Science

Ironically, amidst this technological chaos and loss of sovereignty, basic human science is taking a step forward. Scientists have recently declared a solution to the long-standing "proton size puzzle" in physics, referring to it as the "final nail in the coffin." The fact that we have finally accurately measured the fundamental particles of the universe demonstrates the resilience of human intellect amidst the technological storm.

### The Ethical Boundaries of Technological Autonomy

However, we must ask: Just as we have measured the size of protons, are we accurately measuring the ethical boundaries of the technological autonomy we are expanding? The robots on the battlefield, the self-judging AI, and the satellites and browsers controlled by giant corporations are all racing towards an expansion of autonomy. But this autonomy is not for human freedom; it is for technological systems and capital power to escape human control.

### The Final Stronghold of Humanity

We must confront the digital ownership and decision-making rights we are giving up, sedated by the sweet anesthesia of convenience. The "human uniqueness" felt by the Artemis II astronauts in the solitude of space is the last stronghold we must protect amidst the waves of technological singularity. True technological progress is not completed by the mere enhancement of machine intelligence; it is when human ethics and wisdom mature alongside this intelligence. Only then can we prevent the end of sovereignty imposed by technological capitalism. The turning point of 2026 warns us: as we hand over autonomy to machines, we must resist more fiercely to protect the definition of humanity.
