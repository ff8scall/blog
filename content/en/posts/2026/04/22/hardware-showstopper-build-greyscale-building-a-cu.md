## Executive Summary
- The "Greyscale" project showcases a custom liquid-cooled ITX build designed to overcome the thermal density of high-performance components. By optimizing radiator surface area and coolant flow in a restricted chassis, the build proves that small-form-factor PCs can handle extreme workloads. This technical benchmark emphasizes precision engineering in cooling architectural layouts.

## Strategic Deep-Dive

The "Greyscale" build is an exercise in extreme volumetric efficiency. As a systems architect, the primary challenge in small-form-factor (SFF) design is the management of thermal density—modern GPUs and CPUs generate more heat per cubic inch than ever before. This project bypasses the limitations of traditional air cooling by implementing a meticulously designed custom liquid loop.

The build likely utilizes a "sandwich" internal layout to isolate the GPU from the CPU thermal zone, paired with ultra-slim radiators and high-static-pressure fans. Every fitting and tube run was calculated to avoid air pockets and maximize laminar flow within the coolant channels. By successfully cooling components previously deemed "uncoolable" in this form factor, Greyscale sets a new standard for high-performance mini-workstations, proving that engineering precision can overcome physical space constraints.
