## Strategic Deep-Dive

### Flight to Safety and the Dilemma of Monetary Policy

Amidst increasing uncertainty, investment capital is rapidly shifting from risky assets such as stocks to safe havens like the dollar and gold. This capital outflow is exacerbating the depreciation of Asian currencies, creating a vicious cycle that fuels import price inflation. Central banks across the region find themselves in a profound dilemma.

Raising interest rates to combat inflation is hampered by concerns of economic stagnation, while lowering rates to stimulate the economy is constrained by the price shocks emanating from the Middle East. Ultimately, the volatility in Asian stock markets is not merely a numerical fluctuation; it is a real-time economic indicator demonstrating how geopolitical conflicts can paralyze even the capillaries of the global real economy.
