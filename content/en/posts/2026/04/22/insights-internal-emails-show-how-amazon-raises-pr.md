## Executive Summary
- Leaked internal emails reveal Amazon’s systematic pressure on vendors to eliminate discounts on rival platforms.
* The phrase "It’s working!" highlights the success of these anti-competitive pricing strategies.
* A major lawsuit alleges this created an artificial "price floor," harming global e-commerce transparency.

## Strategic Deep-Dive

The unfolding legal battle against Amazon has exposed a dark underbelly of algorithmic price management. Internal emails produced in discovery reveal a coordinated effort to force vendors into raising prices on non-Amazon platforms. By threatening to lower the search visibility of products that were found cheaper elsewhere, Amazon effectively incentivized a "disappearing deals" phenomenon.

This ensures that Amazon’s listings appear competitive not through organic price cuts, but through the forced inflation of the entire market's baseline.

This practice, described in internal correspondence with the celebratory phrase "It’s working!", suggests a calculated approach to "platform rent-seeking." Instead of competing on service or efficiency, Amazon leveraged its gatekeeper status to dictate a global price floor. This has profound implications for antitrust enforcement in the digital age. If proven, it demonstrates how a dominant platform can distort the core mechanism of the free market—price discovery—replacing it with a centralized, managed economy that penalizes smaller retailers for offering better value to consumers.
