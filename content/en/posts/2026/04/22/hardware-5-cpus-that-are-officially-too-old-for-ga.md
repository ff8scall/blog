## Executive Summary
- As game engines in 2026 mandate advanced instruction sets and AI-processing capabilities, five legendary CPUs—including the i7-10700K and Ryzen 5 3600—have officially hit their technical end-of-life for modern gaming. This report analyzes why these processors can no longer sustain acceptable performance.

## Strategic Deep-Dive

The year 2026 has introduced a harsh reality for PC enthusiasts: the "infinite lifespan" of high-end CPUs is a myth. As developers pivot toward engines that rely heavily on AI-assisted upscaling (DLSS 4/FSR 5) and real-time path tracing, the architectural limitations of older chips have become insurmountable. This year’s benchmarks have officially identified five specific processors that are no longer viable for a stable 60FPS experience in modern titles.

## The Obsolete Five: A Technical Breakdown

The list of CPUs entering forced retirement includes the Intel Core i7-10700K, Core i5-10400, Core i9-9900K, and AMD’s Ryzen 7 3700X and Ryzen 5 3600. While these were the champions of the 2019-2021 era, they lack the specific instructional sets—most notably AVX-512 and integrated NPU (Neural Processing Unit) handoff capabilities—that 2026 games require to manage complex NPC AI and physics calculations. In our tests, these CPUs caused massive frame-time spikes and, in some cases, failed to launch titles that require 12+ thread efficiency as a baseline.

## The New "Hardware Floor" for 2026

We are witnessing the death of the 4-core and entry-level 6-core era. In 2026, the minimum requirement for a fluid gaming experience has shifted to an 8-core, 16-thread architecture with a focus on cache latency rather than raw clock speed. For those still clinging to these five processors, the message from the industry is clear: you are not just losing frame rates; you are losing access to the newest generation of interactive media.
