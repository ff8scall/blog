## Strategic Deep-Dive

### Technical Challenges: Power Supply Design and the Rise of the "Silicon Lottery"

This policy shift extends beyond simply unlocking software features, promising to generate technical repercussions throughout the hardware ecosystem. Firstly, the ability to optimize performance even in the i5 and i3 budget lineups is expected to reignite the "Silicon Lottery" as a key topic of discussion among users. Particularly with budget silicon, power efficiency and thermal characteristics are likely to be less uniform compared to high-end dies.

This disparity will likely result in fierce benchmarking competitions among enthusiasts seeking to identify stable, usable clock speeds.

Furthermore, this presents new challenges for motherboard manufacturers. Historically, lower-cost B-series and H-series motherboards have adopted weak Voltage Regulator Module (VRM) designs, not intended for overclocking. However, securing voltage stability through enhanced designs will become essential, even for entry-level chipsets.

Intel appears to be aiming to broaden the demand for hardware acceleration from high-end systems to the mainstream market as a whole.
