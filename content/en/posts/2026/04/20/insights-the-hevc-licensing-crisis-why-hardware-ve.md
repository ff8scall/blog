## Executive Summary
- Hardware vendors are increasingly dropping support for HEVC due to complex royalty structures and legal risks, signaling a shift toward royalty-free alternatives like AV1.

## Strategic Deep-Dive

The High-Efficiency Video Coding (HEVC/H.265) standard, once hailed as the future of 4K and 8K video, is facing a significant exodus from hardware vendors. The primary driver is the convoluted licensing landscape. Unlike its predecessor H.264, HEVC is governed by multiple patent pools—including MPEG LA, Access Advance, and Velos Media—leading to cumulative royalty fees that thin the margins of consumer electronics.

This "economic friction" has reached a tipping point where the cost of implementation outweighs the technical benefits. Furthermore, the rise of royalty-free alternatives like AV1 has provided a viable escape path for companies looking to avoid perpetual litigation and opaque billing practices. As vendors prioritize profit stability, the industry is witnessing a strategic retreat from HEVC in favor of more open standards.
