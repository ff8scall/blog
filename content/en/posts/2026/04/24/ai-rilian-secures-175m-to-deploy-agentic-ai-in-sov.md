## Executive Summary
- McLean-based Rilian has raised $17.5 million to scale its 'Caspian' platform, focusing on deploying pre-trained AI agents in air-gapped, compliance-restricted defense sectors.

## Strategic Deep-Dive

Rilian, a defense-focused startup headquartered in McLean, Virginia, has successfully raised $17.5 million to bridge the gap between cutting-edge generative AI and the stringent requirements of national security. The company's flagship product, the Caspian platform, functions as a high-level command layer integrated into existing security infrastructures. Unlike consumer-facing AI that mandates continuous cloud connectivity, Caspian is purpose-built to deploy pre-trained AI agents into air-gapped environments—networks that are physically isolated from the open internet to prevent data exfiltration.

The technical challenge addressed by Rilian is the operationalization of "Agentic AI" in sectors where data sovereignty is a matter of survival. In compliance-restricted environments, traditional AI models fail due to their reliance on external APIs and centralized computing. Rilian’s architecture allows for localized, high-performance inference and agent orchestration, enabling military and intelligence operators to execute complex analytical tasks without risking their most sensitive assets.

The inclusion of co-founder Nick Pompeo, son of former U.S. Secretary of State Mike Pompeo, underscores the firm's strategic alignment with the highest levels of the defense establishment. Rilian is a prime example of the emerging 'Sovereign AI' movement, where the focus shifts from general-purpose flexibility to specialized, secure, and independent infrastructure designed for national defense.
