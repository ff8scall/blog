## Executive Summary
- BT Group and Nscale are repurposing legacy telecom sites into 14MW high-density AI data centers in collaboration with NVIDIA.
- The partnership is a cornerstone of the 'Sovereign AI' movement, ensuring the UK retains localized compute capacity.
- As founding members of the UK Sovereign AI Industry Forum, the companies will align infrastructure with national security and digital resilience goals.

## Strategic Deep-Dive

In a move to strengthen the United Kingdom's technological independence, BT Group and Nscale have partnered with NVIDIA to establish a dedicated Sovereign AI infrastructure. This collaboration involves the construction of 14MW of high-density data center capacity by repurposing existing BT telecommunications sites. By leveraging legacy infrastructure, the partnership can rapidly deploy AI compute nodes with superior connectivity and lower latency across the nation.

This initiative is deeply integrated with the newly formed UK Sovereign AI Industry Forum, where BT and Nscale serve as founding members. The strategic goal is to ensure that the UK possesses the localized compute power necessary to foster a homegrown AI ecosystem while maintaining strict data residency and security standards.

Nscale’s role focuses on the deployment of NVIDIA-powered clusters tailored for generative AI and large-scale data processing. By utilizing BT’s extensive network fabric, the partnership ensures that high-performance compute is accessible to UK enterprises and government bodies without relying on offshore cloud providers. This alignment with the UK government’s national AI infrastructure strategy underscores the importance of 'Sovereign AI' in maintaining digital resilience and national security.

Repurposing brownfield telecom sites for AI workloads also addresses the growing scarcity of power and land for traditional data center developments, showcasing an efficient model for national digital transformation.
